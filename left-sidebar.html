Competition

Prompt

You are part of the Environmental Protection Agency. Given recent environmental developments, like the California wildfires and Hurricane Irma, finding an effective STEM solution to solve either local or international issues is critical. Please choose a specific problem, such as water purification, plastic biodegradation, etc. Assume you have full power and control over your solution (i.e. unlimited materials). Present your projected conclusions.

How to Compete

Read the prompt and steps on the left, and the official guidelines below.

Official Guidelines

1. Project entries may be theoretical or actually designed and executed.
2. Projects must fit in the space of a tri-fold poster board.
3. Projects must be present on the day of the event in order to be considered.
4. Projects may not involve non-human vertebrates.
5. Projects involving humans may only do so for psychological or behavior studies.
6. Projects previously submitted for science fair projects will be judged in a separate category.
7. If researching people under 18 years of age, parental consent is required.
8. Participants are responsible for any material fees, project set-up, and project clean-up.

Judging

Our keynote speakers and panelists (all STEM professionals) will be judging the projects and determining a first, second, and third place winner.

Prizes

Prizes will include Amazon gift cards and official BARE certificates.

2017 1st Place

Lynbrook juniors Kuhu Mathur, Hsinyen Huang, and Anubha Kale created an app prototype to combate stress in school. They planned out what the different screens and functions of the app would like, and presented their proposed design.
